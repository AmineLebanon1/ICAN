Frequently Asked Questions

Sign language is a visual language that uses hand gestures, facial expressions, and body movements to communicate. It is used by deaf and hard of hearing individuals to convey thoughts, ideas, and emotions.

Levantine Sign Language is used primarily in the Levant region of the Middle East, including countries like Syria, Lebanon, Jordan, and parts of Palestine. It is used by deaf individuals and their communities as a means of communication and expression.

Sign language is a visual-gestural language that uses hand shapes, movements, and facial expressions to convey meaning, while spoken language relies on sounds and vocalizations. Sign languages are fully-fledged languages with their own grammar, syntax, and vocabulary, and they are not simply a visual representation of spoken languages.

A sign language translator is a device or software that converts spoken or written language into sign language, or vice versa, to facilitate communication between deaf or hard of hearing individuals and those who do not use sign language.

Sign language translators work by using sensors or cameras to capture sign language gestures, which are then processed and translated into spoken or written language, or vice versa, using artificial intelligence and machine learning algorithms.

Sign language translators can provide instant access to communication for deaf or hard of hearing individuals, increase inclusivity in various settings, and improve the efficiency of communication.
